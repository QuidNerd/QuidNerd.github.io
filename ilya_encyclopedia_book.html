Physical Description
Average height, average build. Two-tone curly hair, dyed black on top while the bottom half remains its original blond (a whim abandoned halfway as it was too much trouble). Pale, undernourished skin. Dark undereye circles. Freckles across the nose and cheeks. His fine features showed the clear advantage of excellent genetics, though those blue-hazel eyes were permanently half-lidded and vacant; the overall effect being that of a young boy who appeared to have been drained of all life force before anyone had bothered to put any in.

Mental Description
At some point in his childhood, Ilya arrived at the conclusion that most things are not worth the effort they require and has been acting on it ever since. He is not depressed in any clinical sense; he's just very, very lazy and has never had enough reason to be otherwise.
He can often be found doing the bare minimum at the last possible moment, when it gets done at all. (Except when a little effort now guarantees absolute laziness later; his hatred of trouble is the only thing that can force him to be temporarily useful.) The things his family considers motivating, such as legacy and reputation and the constant performance of excellence, have never produced in him the intended effect, which is probably related to the fact that none of it was ever directed at him to begin with.
He is likewise of the opinion that if you wait long enough, most situations resolve without your input, or simply stop being situations at all. So far this has held, and on the rare occasions it didn't, he grudgingly squashed the nuisance just to get it out of his sight.

II. History & First Instance of Magic
[landscape photo]
Fig. 5 — Veliky Novgorod
Based in Veliky Novgorod, the Voronov family has maintained its status and pure bloodline for eight centuries. Ilya Konstantinovich Voronov is the fourthborn of the current patriarch. In a family of this size, parental attention is a finite resource that has never once been distributed evenly in the history of human parenting; our unfortunate boy Ilya had not only lost the birth-order lottery, but had done so while being unremarkably average. He had none of his eldest brother's authority, none of his second sister's charisma, and none of his third brother's raw talent; whatever remained of his parents' regard had also been thoroughly monopolized by his youngest brother, who was coddled by every member of the household down to the house-elves.
For all practical purposes, Ilya was the superfluous one. Лишний человек.
Nobody could legally sue the Voronovs for child neglect, as Ilya had been very well taken care of materially, he had a massive bedroom, private tutors, and a butler who would procure him anything if Ilya harassed him enough times. But he had grown up entirely within this world and had no frame of reference outside of it, so the idea that he was privileged never quite registered. What he did notice eventually was whether he registered at all, the answer was mostly no.
If he threw a tantrum for attention, it was ignored; if he stayed quiet, it was a relief to a busy household. Any practical need or inconvenience was so efficiently absorbed by the servants that it never needed to travel up to his parents' ears. Even at the family dinner table, when the children were expected to recount the day's accomplishments, his contributions never measured up to those of his siblings, thus earning him no praise. Ilya quickly realized that competing with his siblings for attention was simply pointless, so he gave up and withdrew into the background, ending in a state of learned helplessness, asking for nothing and offering nothing.
As for Koldovstoretz over Durmstrang, his older siblings all went to the latter, so he'd rather not.

First Instance of Magic
At nine years old, Ilya's first instance of magic went entirely unnoticed. He had been sitting alone in an unused room feeling uncomfortably warm when a rusted window, stuck for years, simply slid open. He mentioned it to his parents, who agreed it was probably magic and changed the subject fairly quickly. Ilya went back to the room, and the window stayed open.
